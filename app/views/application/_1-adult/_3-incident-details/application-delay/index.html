Help with why we ask about the application not being sent until now

We must receive your application as soon as reasonably practicable and, no later than 2 years after the date of the crime.

The 2-year window is extendable where exceptional circumstances show why you could not apply sooner. For example, you may have delayed sending the application because:

  • you were under 18 at the time of the crime
  • the impact of the injuries stopped you from applying sooner

You may have to provide evidence to support any reason for a delay in applying. This evidence can include a letter from your GP or other medical professionals.

Any evidence to support your application should:

  • show why there was a delay for the entire 2-year period if you're applying 2 years after the crime has taken place
  • be provided by someone in a professional capacity, such as a psychologist or GP

By providing supporting evidence, the application may be determined without further extensive enquiries.
