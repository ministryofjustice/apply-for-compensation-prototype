Apply for compensation for a criminal injury

{{releaseVersion | log }}

This prototype details iterations of the application process users will go through to apply for compensation if they have suffered an injury through being a victim of violent crime.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

Our MVP will cover users who have non-complex sexual assault applications.

Current versionMVP Sprint 2

Following user research we are iterating the design of the MVP service.

Delay reporting (more than 48h) is included now. You will be asked for a reason why you delayed reporting using :

  • the day of the incident if it's a single incident
  • the last day of the month of the end of the period of abuse otherwise

Release 0.6last updated 11/06/2018

This version is for usability testing on 12/06/2018 in Glasgow.

The focus of this journey is to see how simple we can make the application process by getting all but the simplest users out of the journey as early as possible.

Users that do not fall in to the simple group will be nudged in other directions. We have yet to define those other directions.

This journey focuses on users who:

  • are direct applicants
  • are over 18
  • are British citizens
  • do not have any criminal convictions
  • have applied for compensation from other sources
  • do not have ongoing medical treatment
  • have suffered personal injury

This release contains:

  • refinded content for most pages
  • removed question about obtaining a medical report
  • changes the stepped guide to bring it in line with the start page
