Claim compensation if you were the victim of a violent crime

Make a new claim

You can claim for compensation online.

When you make a claim to the Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (CICA), you may need to provide:

  • the date and location of the crime
  • the name of the police station where the crime was reported
  • the crime reference number
  • GP’s name and address
  • dentist’s name and address (if dental treatment was needed because of the injuries)
  • details of any previous applications you’ve made to CICA
  • details of any unspent criminal convictions
! Warning If you deliberately provide information you know is wrong or misleading, you may be prosecuted and the application for compensation may be refused.

You’ll be asked if you’ve tried to get compensation or other money you, or the person you're representing, may be entitled to, for example:

  • by claiming benefits
  • through insurance payments
  • from a civil court claim
  • from a criminal court case (if the crime went to court)

You do not need to wait for the outcome of other claims before applying.

It does not cost anything to apply and you do not have to use a legal adviser.

Save an application

After answering a few initial questions, you can save your application progress and submit it later.

  • do not have internet access

General enquiries

Email info@cica.gov.uk if you have a question about the claims process.
