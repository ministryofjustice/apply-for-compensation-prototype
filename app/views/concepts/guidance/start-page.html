If you are handling a telephone application you do not need to read all of the content on this page to users.

To reduce ineligible applications we tell users that the crime must have been reported to the police. You can tell them to contact 101 to report it.

Advise users of the organisations they can apply to if the crime did not happen in England, Scotland, Wales, or another relevant place. For example Northern Ireland or EUCAT.

Tell users what information they need before they start as they will not be able to ‘save and return’.

Victims of sexual assault or abuse often apply outside the 2 year limit. Reassure them that we can make exceptions in certain circumstances.

Direct users to the online service where possible. However, we should offer the telephone application services to support users with assisted digital needs.

Signpost users to support services if you identify that they need practical help or emotional support.
