You’ve told us the person you’re applying for is unable to handle their own affairs in relation to this application.

Therefore, we’re going to ask for details about the person who has legal authority for the victim.

This may be a person:

  • appointed by a court
  • named on power of attorney documentation
  • who works for the local authority
Can I apply if I do not have legal authority?

You can still apply even if you do not have legal authority for the victim. This means you have to provide the details of who has authority for them in this application. This allows us to communicate with the person who has authority after you’ve sent this application.

{% else %}

We’re going to ask for details about the person who has parental responsibility for the child. This means they have the legal authority to apply on their behalf.

This may be:

  • the child’s birth, step or adoptive parent
  • a person named on a Special Guardianship order
  • someone who works for the local authority
Can I apply if I do not have parental responsibility?

You can still apply even if you do not have parental responsibility for the child. This means you have to provide the details of who has authority for the child in order to send this application. This allows us to communicate with this person or organisation after you’ve sent this application.
