Use this service to claim compensation if:

  • you were the victim of a sexual assault or abuse
  • you suffered mental injuries because of a violent crime
  • you suffered physical injuries because of a violent crime
  • you saw a violent crime happen to a loved one, or were there immediately afterwards
  • a close relative died because of a violent crime
  • you paid for the funeral of someone who died because of a violent crime

You cannot get compensation if the crime has not been reported to the police.

The crime must have happened in England, Wales, Scotland or another relevant place.

There is a different service for Northern Ireland.

Start now

Before you start

Some of the things you'll need are:

  • the crime reference number
  • the approximate date that the crime happened
  • the name of the police force that's dealing with the crime
  • the location where the crime happened
  • an email address

Compensation is paid by the government, not the offender. We may be able to pay compensation even if the offender is not identified or convicted.

Eligibility

Usually you must apply within 2 years of when the crime happened. An exception may be made if one or both of the following applies:

  • you were under 18 at the time
  • you can show that you could not apply earlier

Other ways to apply

You can contact us for help with your application on 0300 003 3601. Select option 8.

Our phone lines are open Monday to Friday 8:30am to 5pm except Wednesday when they open at 10am.

Help and support

You can get practical or emotional support depending on where you live:

  • in England and Wales visit the Victim and Witness Information website
  • in Scotland visit the mygov.scot website
